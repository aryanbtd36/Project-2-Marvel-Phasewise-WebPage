The Marvel Cinematic Universe (MCU) is an interconnected franchise of superhero films and series produced by Marvel Studios. What began with Iron Man in 2008 has evolved into a vast, multi-phase saga spanning decades, galaxies, and realities. Each phase brings new heroes, deeper storylines, and epic crossovers that redefine cinematic storytelling. This site presents the MCU in chronological order—not by release date, but by the timeline of events within the universe itself. Whether you're a first-time viewer or a long-time fan revisiting the saga, this guide will help you experience the MCU as the story unfolds within its own world.

The Marvel Cinematic Universe (MCU) is divided into story arcs called Phases. Each Phase groups together a series of interconnected movies (and now shows), building toward major crossover events and reshaping the future of the franchise.
Below is a quick breakdown of each Phase:

Phase 1 (2008–2012)

The Beginning of Heroes Introduced the core Avengers: Iron Man, Captain America, Thor, and more. Culminated in the first major crossover: The Avengers (2012).
